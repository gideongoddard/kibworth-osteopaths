Coronavirus

Hello and I hope you're well.

I wanted to let you all know that I am open, and able see patients. The only patients I cannot see are those who have a positive Covid-19 status. The surgery looks and feels a little different, as I have removed everything that doesn't need to be there, to ensure maximum cleanliness. I also look a little different in my PPE. I can assure you I am smiling under the mask and visor!

I want to reassure you that I have made the clinic as safe as possible. These are the procedures that are in place:

  • All patients are screened the day before their appointment with questions relating to Covid-19.
  • Patients are asked to wash their hands upon arrival to the clinic.
  • I am asking where possible that patients attend their appointments alone, unless the appointment is for a child or vulnerable adult, in which case I ask that only ONE chaperone attend.
  • I wear full PPE throughout the appointment (mask, visor, apron etc) which is changed for every patient.
  • Full and thorough hygiene measures are in place, including but not limited to:
    • Thorough cleaning of the treatment room and waiting areas with Chlor-Clean in between patients.
    • New disposable plinth covers (changed for every patient)
    • Waterproof and antibacterial pillow protectors (changed for every patient).

How it works

New patients will usually have an initial appointment for consultation and treatment lasting around 1 hour, with any subsequent appointments for treatment lasting around 30 minutes.

Here's how you can expect things to work:

Case history

A case history is taken about your symptoms so that the Osteopath has a complete understanding of what has happened, why, and when.

Then a full medical history about your general medical is taken so that you are kept safe throughout. This also helps the Osteopath understand you as a whole person, not just the symptomatic part of you.

Physical examination

An examination of the area that is causing problems is needed so wearing gym clothing is ideal.

Osteopaths are trained to examine areas of the body using a highly developed sense of touch to identify the body's points of weakness or excessive strain.

Explanation

It will then be explained to you what the injury is, why it has happened, and a treatment plan will be discussed. Treatment plans usually consist of short, medium and long term goals, and these are unique to each patient.

Informed patient consent is very important to me, and this is an on-going process. No treatment will ever be given without a through explanation of what can be done and how - i.e. the techniques that would be most safe and effective for you.

Treatment

During the initial appointment, treatment is usually provided using a combination of soft-tissue release work for tight muscles, tendons or ligaments, specific joint mobilisation techniques and much more.

Advice on self-help is provided to help keep the recovery process active - e.g. stretches, ice or heat, ergonomic advice.

Any subsequent treatments are then 30 minutes, unless an injury is complex or there are multiple injury sites, then more time may be required.

Self-help advice is regularly updated to keep the recovery process moving forward as injury moves towards total recovery. Advice is given on what can be done to keep symptom reoccurrace risk at a minimum.
